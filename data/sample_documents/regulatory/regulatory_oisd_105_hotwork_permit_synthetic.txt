CHAPTER 1 — SCOPE AND APPLICATION

1.1 This standard specifies minimum requirements for the Permit to Work (PTW) system
    applicable to all hot work operations conducted in oil and gas installations, refineries,
    petrochemical plants, and other hazardous area facilities in India.

1.2 Hot work, for the purpose of this standard, includes: welding, gas cutting, plasma
    cutting, grinding (that produces sparks), operation of internal combustion engines
    in hazardous areas, and any other work that may produce heat, sparks, or flame.

1.3 This standard shall be read in conjunction with OISD-STD-116 (Fire Protection
    Facilities), OISD-STD-144 (Liquefied Petroleum Gas), and the applicable provisions
    of the Factories Act, 1948 and the Petroleum Act, 1934.

CHAPTER 2 — DEFINITIONS

2.1 Hot Work Area: Any area where hot work is to be performed.

2.2 Hazardous Area: An area where flammable or explosive atmospheres may exist.

2.3 Lower Explosive Limit (LEL): The minimum concentration of a flammable gas or vapour
    in air below which the mixture cannot be ignited.

2.4 Permit Issuing Authority (PIA): The person authorised to issue a hot work permit,
    typically the area supervisor or safety officer. The PIA must have completed hot work
    permit training and be assessed as competent.

2.5 Permit Receiver: The person performing or supervising the hot work.

CHAPTER 3 — GENERAL REQUIREMENTS FOR HOT WORK PERMITS

3.1 No hot work shall be performed without a valid Hot Work Permit issued by the
    Permit Issuing Authority.

3.2 A Hot Work Permit shall be valid for one shift only (maximum 12 hours). If work
    extends beyond one shift, a fresh permit must be issued.

3.3 The Permit Issuing Authority shall conduct a physical inspection of the work area
    before issuing the permit.

3.4 The Hot Work Permit shall specify:
    (a) Location of work (area, equipment tag, elevation)
    (b) Nature of hot work to be performed
    (c) Duration of permit validity
    (d) Gas test results at time of permit issuance
    (e) Name and signature of Permit Issuing Authority
    (f) Name and signature of Permit Receiver
    (g) Fire extinguisher location and standby arrangements
    (h) Conditions under which hot work must be immediately stopped

CHAPTER 4 — GAS TESTING REQUIREMENTS

4.1 GAS TEST BEFORE PERMIT ISSUANCE

4.1.1 A gas test shall be performed in the hot work area immediately before permit
      issuance. The gas test must be conducted by a competent person using a calibrated
      portable gas detector.

4.1.2 Hot work shall NOT be permitted if the combustible gas concentration in the
      work area exceeds 10% LEL.

4.1.3 The gas test result (equipment ID, reading in % LEL, time, and tester's name)
      shall be recorded on the Hot Work Permit form. The field MUST NOT be left blank
      or marked "N/A". If gas testing equipment is unavailable, hot work shall not proceed.

4.1.4 Where fixed gas detectors are installed in the hot work area, the current reading
      from the nearest fixed detector shall also be recorded on the permit. A fixed detector
      reading above 10% LEL shall prevent permit issuance regardless of portable test result.

4.2 GAS TESTING DURING HOT WORK

4.2.1 Continuous monitoring of the work area shall be maintained during hot work using
      either fixed detectors or a dedicated portable monitor operated by a standby person.

4.2.2 Hot work shall be IMMEDIATELY suspended if:
      (a) Any gas reading in the work area exceeds 10% LEL.
      (b) Any fixed detector within 15 metres of the work area reads above 10% LEL.
      (c) An odour of gas, smoke, or unusual vapour is detected.
      (d) Any fire, spark, or unexpected heat source is observed.

4.3 FREQUENCY OF GAS READINGS

4.3.1 Gas readings shall be recorded at minimum every 30 minutes during hot work and
      logged on the permit form or a dedicated monitoring log.

4.3.2 Where a fixed gas detector (such as a permanent combustible gas monitor installed
      per relevant equipment standards) is present within 10 metres of the hot work, the
      fixed detector reading shall be read and logged at minimum every 30 minutes.

4.3.3 If a fixed detector alarm activates during hot work, hot work shall be suspended
      immediately and not resumed until the alarm cause has been investigated and cleared
      by the Permit Issuing Authority.

4.4 GAS DETECTOR CALIBRATION

4.4.1 Portable gas detectors used for hot work permit gas testing shall be calibrated
      per the manufacturer's recommendation, at minimum every 6 months, and bump-tested
      before each use.

4.4.2 Fixed gas detectors protecting hot work areas shall be calibrated per manufacturer
      recommendations. A fixed detector that is overdue for calibration shall be treated
      as non-functional. Hot work in areas protected solely by overdue fixed detectors
      shall not proceed until calibration is completed.

4.4.3 Calibration records for all gas detection equipment shall be maintained and available
      for inspection. Records shall include: calibration date, technician name, reference
      gas certificate number, pre-calibration reading, and post-calibration reading.

CHAPTER 5 — RESPONSIBILITIES

5.1 FACILITY MANAGEMENT
    (a) Ensure a written Hot Work Permit procedure is in place and reviewed annually.
    (b) Ensure all Permit Issuing Authorities are trained and assessed as competent.
    (c) Ensure adequate gas detection equipment is available and maintained.

5.2 PERMIT ISSUING AUTHORITY
    (a) Conduct physical inspection before issuing any permit.
    (b) Record all gas readings on the permit form — never leave gas reading fields blank.
    (c) Suspend hot work immediately on any gas alarm or elevated reading.
    (d) Ensure hot work is fully completed or safely abandoned before permit closure.

5.3 PERMIT RECEIVER
    (a) Understand the conditions and limitations of the permit before commencing work.
    (b) Stop work immediately if any of the suspension conditions in 4.2.2 arise.
    (c) Not commence hot work before the permit is signed and in hand.

CHAPTER 6 — LESSONS LEARNED AND PERMIT SYSTEM REVIEW

6.1 All hot work incidents and near-misses shall be reviewed to identify gaps in the
    permit system.

6.2 Where the same location or equipment tag appears in two or more hot work related
    incidents or near-misses within a 5-year period, the facility shall:
    (a) Conduct a formal investigation of the recurring pattern.
    (b) Review whether the hot work procedure for that specific area is adequate.
    (c) Consider whether additional engineering controls (interlocks, additional monitoring)
        are warranted.

6.2.4 Recurring gas alarm events: If a fixed detector in a hot work area triggers at
      >15% LEL on two or more occasions while a hot work permit is active, the facility's
      permit procedures for that area shall be formally reviewed within 30 days and updated
      with additional safeguards. The lessons learned shall be shared with all permit
      issuing authorities.

CHAPTER 7 — DOCUMENTATION

7.1 Completed Hot Work Permits shall be retained for a minimum of 3 years.
7.2 A register of all hot work permits issued shall be maintained.
7.3 Gas test records associated with hot work permits shall be retained with the permit.
