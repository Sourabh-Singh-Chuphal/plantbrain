───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. INCIDENT DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
At 14:32 on 14 March 2019, equipment tag GB-14 (combustible gas detector installed at Bay 4,
Zone C, at elevation 3.2m) registered a sustained alarm reading of 27% LEL (Lower Explosive
Limit). The reading had been gradually rising from 11% LEL since 12:15 IST but was not
escalated by the shift operator (Suresh Kumar, Operator-II) until it crossed the 25% LEL
threshold trigger.

At the time of the alarm, a hot work permit (Permit No. HWP-2019-112) had been issued for
gas cutting operations being carried out by contractor technician Balraj Tiwari (Sarwa
Contractors Pvt. Ltd.) approximately 8 metres from the GB-14 sensor location. The permit had
been authorised by Rajiv Sharma (Maintenance Supervisor) at 13:45 IST.

On receiving the alarm escalation, HSE Officer Priya Nair immediately issued a STOP WORK
order. All hot work was halted. The area was ventilated using forced-draft blowers for 45
minutes. A second gas reading at 15:20 showed 4% LEL. Normal operations resumed at 16:10.

No fire, explosion, or injury occurred. This event is classified as a NEAR-MISS — Category 2
(High Potential).

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. IMMEDIATE CAUSES
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
(a) GB-14 sensor had not been calibrated since 22 September 2018 (last calibration record:
    Work Order WO-3591, Technician: Anil Mehta). The manufacturer (Draeger) recommends
    6-monthly calibration. At the time of this incident the sensor was 174 days overdue.

(b) Operator Suresh Kumar did not escalate the rising gas reading (11%→27% LEL over 137
    minutes) per the Shift Handover Procedure SHP-04 which requires supervisor notification
    at 15% LEL.

(c) Hot Work Permit HWP-2019-112 was issued without a pre-work gas reading check from
    GB-14, contrary to OISD-STD-105 Clause 4.3.1 which mandates a fresh gas reading within
    30 minutes of hot work commencement.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. ROOT CAUSES
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
(a) No automated system exists to block hot work permit issuance when nearby gas sensor
    readings exceed 10% LEL. Permits are currently issued on paper without cross-checking
    live sensor data.

(b) Calibration tracking is manual (maintained in a paper logbook in the Maintenance
    Supervisor's office). No digital reminder or work order auto-generation exists when
    calibration is overdue.

(c) Operator training records show Suresh Kumar last attended gas hazard escalation training
    in February 2017 — over 2 years prior to this incident.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. CORRECTIVE ACTIONS ISSUED
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CA-01 | GB-14 to be calibrated within 72 hours. (Assigned: Anil Mehta. Due: 17 Mar 2019)
CA-02 | All hot work permits in Bay 4 to require a live gas reading sign-off from the nearest
       sensor (GB-14 or GB-17) within 30 minutes of permit issuance. (Immediate)
CA-03 | Suresh Kumar to attend refresher training — Gas Hazard Response, Module 3.
       (Due: 31 March 2019)
CA-04 | Maintenance department to propose a digital calibration tracking system with
       auto-generated work orders. (Due: 30 June 2019)
CA-05 | Incident to be shared at next All-Hands Safety Meeting and added to lessons-learned
       register. Permit no. HWP-2019-112 to be flagged in HSEQ system. (Due: 22 March 2019)
