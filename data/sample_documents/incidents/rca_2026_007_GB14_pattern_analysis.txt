1. EVENT DESCRIPTION (SUMMARY)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
On 21 January 2026 at 23:15 IST, fixed gas detector GB-14 (Bay 4, Zone C) registered
22% LEL methane while Hot Work Permit HWP-2026-018 was active for plasma cutting on
PV-08 in Bay 4. Operator Suresh Kumar suspended hot work at 23:15. Investigation found
methane source: HX-11 Flange #7 gasket failure. Area cleared by 06:15 on 22 January.

This is the THIRD occurrence of this scenario — elevated GB-14 readings coinciding with
active hot work in Bay 4. Previous events: NMR-2019-047 (March 2019) and WO-4471 (Aug 2024).

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. TIMELINE OF EVENTS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
21 Jan 2026, 21:30 — Hot Work Permit HWP-2026-018 issued by Rajiv Sharma.
                      Gas reading field on permit: "0% — area clear" (no time, no tester).
21 Jan 2026, 22:00 — Shift change. Suresh Kumar takes over from Day Shift.
                      GB-14 reading at handover: 7% LEL (noted in SHL-2026-0121).

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS (5-WHY METHOD)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PROBLEM STATEMENT: GB-14 triggered at 22% LEL while hot work permit HWP-2026-018 was
active in Bay 4, creating risk of ignition.

Why 1: Why was there methane at 22% LEL in Bay 4?
Answer: HX-11 Flange #7 gasket failed, allowing methane from the process stream to
        accumulate in Bay 4.

Why 2: Why did HX-11 Flange #7 gasket fail?
Answer: The gasket had progressive degradation from thermal cycling. Seepage was first
        identified in WO-4476 (August 2024) and was NOT permanently repaired — only
        bolts were retorqued. No formal work order for gasket replacement was raised.

Why 3: Why was gasket replacement not completed after WO-4476?
Answer: WO-4476 was closed as "partial" with only a verbal instruction to raise a
        follow-up WO at "next planned shutdown." No formal WO was raised. The 2025
        Annual Safety Audit (SAR-2025) flagged this as open risk but it was not escalated.

Why 4: Why was the hot work permit (HWP-2026-018) issued despite the known HX-11 seepage risk?
Answer: Rajiv Sharma was not aware that the HX-11 gasket replacement was still outstanding.
        There is no system linking open maintenance risks to permit issuance checks.
        Additionally, the gas reading on the permit was recorded without time or tester
        name — it is unclear when (or whether) a fresh gas reading was taken.

Why 5: Why did the same scenario recur three times over 7 years without systemic prevention?
Answer: Each event (2019, 2024, 2026) was treated in isolation. Corrective action CA-04
        from NMR-2019-047 — a digital system to cross-link calibration status, open
        maintenance risks, and permit issuance — was raised in 2019 but NEVER implemented
        due to budget deferral (2019, 2020, 2021, 2022) and personnel change (2023–2025).
        Without this system, the recurring pattern was only recognised by individuals
        with long institutional memory (Rajiv Sharma, Anil Mehta, Suresh Kumar).

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. CONTRIBUTORY FACTORS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CF-1: Permit gas reading procedure was not followed correctly by issuer (Rajiv Sharma).
CF-2: Open corrective actions from prior incidents (CA-04, WO-4476) were not systematically
      tracked or escalated.
CF-3: No automated linkage between open maintenance items and areas where hot work is
      being requested.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
5. CORRECTIVE AND PREVENTIVE ACTIONS (CAPA)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RCA-2026-007-C1 | IMMEDIATE: HX-11 Flange #7 emergency gasket replacement.
                  Status: COMPLETED 24 January 2026 (Anil Mehta, Suresh Kumar).

RCA-2026-007-C2 | Hot Work Permit procedure (HWP-PROC) revised to require BOTH portable
                  gas reading AND nearest fixed detector reading with timestamp and tester
                  name. No "N/A" or "area clear" without both readings documented.
                  Status: COMPLETED 30 January 2026 (Kavita Desai, effective immediately).

RCA-2026-007-C3 | STRATEGIC: Implement a digital Integrated Safety Management System (ISMS)
                  that cross-links:
                    (a) Calibration schedules and overdue alerts for all gas detectors
                    (b) Open maintenance items (especially partial-close WOs)
                    (c) Active permit issuance (permit to check for nearby open gas risks)
                  This is CA-04 from NMR-2019-047, now 7 years overdue.
                  Budget approved by Mohan Rao: Rs. 14 lakhs.
                  Target: System operational by July 2026.
                  Owner: Deepak Singh.

RCA-2026-007-C4 | All-hands lessons-learned session: present the NMR-2019-047 → WO-4471 →
                  WO-4892 pattern to all shift operators, supervisors, and permit issuers.
                  Status: Completed 31 January 2026.

RCA-2026-007-C5 | Suresh Kumar's early recognition of the pattern (acting at 15% rather
                  than waiting for 25% LEL threshold) to be documented as best practice
                  and shared at the lessons-learned session.
