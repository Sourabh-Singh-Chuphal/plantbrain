1. WORK DESCRIPTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
URGENT: Suspension and investigation of gas sensor GB-14 following sustained elevated
reading of 22% LEL recorded during Night Shift on 21 January 2026 (Shift Handover Log
SHL-2026-0121).

A Hot Work Permit (HWP-2026-018) was active in Bay 4, Zone C on 21 January 2026 for
plasma-cutting operations on pressure vessel PV-08 structural support bracket.
The permit was issued at 21:30 IST by Rajiv Sharma. At 23:15 IST, Operator Suresh Kumar
(Night Shift) noted GB-14 reading 22% LEL and suspended hot work.

THIS IS THE THIRD OCCURRENCE of elevated GB-14 readings coinciding with active hot work
in Bay 4. Previous occurrences:
  1. March 2019 — 27% LEL, near-miss, NMR-2019-047
  2. August 2024 — 18% LEL, sensor drift + HX-11 flange seepage, WO-4471

Pattern noted by Rajiv Sharma. Immediate escalation to Plant Manager Mohan Rao and
HSE Manager Kavita Desai at 23:45 IST on 21 January 2026.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (22 JAN 2026)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
01:00 — All hot work in Bay 4 suspended indefinitely pending investigation.
02:30 — Portable multi-gas detector deployed by Anil Mehta. Reading at GB-14 location:
         CH4: 19% LEL, CO: 12 ppm, H2S: 0 ppm. Methane source confirmed present.
05:00 — HX-11 flange joint #7 (same joint flagged in WO-4476, Aug 2024) found to have
         progressive seepage — compression gasket degraded. Emergency isolation actioned.
06:15 — Bay 4 ventilated. Methane at GB-14 location: 2% LEL. Area safe.
07:00 — GB-14 calibration checked. Zero drift: +3.8% LEL. Span: 48% LEL at 50% reference.
         Sensor out of tolerance. Emergency recalibration performed.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. ROOT CAUSE INVESTIGATION (in progress)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Preliminary root causes identified (formal RCA to follow under RCA-2026-007):

(a) HX-11 flange joint #7: Gasket (spiral-wound, 150# ANSI) degraded due to thermal
    cycling. This is the SAME joint identified in WO-4476 (August 2024) where seepage
    was noted. WO-4476 was closed as "monitoring required" without gasket replacement.
    Corrective action from WO-4476 was not completed.

(b) GB-14 sensor element: Last replaced per WO-4473 (November 2024). Replacement was
    overdue 6 months prior (first noted in WO-4471). At 14 months of use, sensor
    is within manufacturer's 24-month replacement interval but calibration drift of
    +3.8% LEL suggests early degradation — possibly due to Bay 4 methane exposure.

(c) Hot Work Permit HWP-2026-018: Issued by Rajiv Sharma without live GB-14 reading check.
    Procedure updated in 2019 (CA-02 from NMR-2019-047) requires live reading within
    30 minutes. Permit paperwork shows the gas reading field was marked "N/A — routine area"
    rather than recording an actual reading. This procedural shortcut has recurred.

(d) Pattern recognition failure: The recurrence of this exact scenario (elevated GB-14 +
    active hot work, Bay 4) across 3 events over 7 years was not identified proactively
    by any system or personnel. Each event was treated as isolated.
