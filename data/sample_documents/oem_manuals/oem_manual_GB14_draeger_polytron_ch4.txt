4.1 INTRODUCTION TO CALIBRATION

The Polytron 2 XP uses a catalytic bead (pellistor) sensor to detect combustible gases.
Accurate detection depends on periodic calibration to compensate for sensor aging, poisoning,
and environmental factors. Failure to calibrate on schedule is the leading cause of false
negatives (failure to alarm when gas is present) and false positives (nuisance alarms).

4.2 CALIBRATION INTERVALS

4.2.1 Standard Calibration Schedule
  - Zero check (bump test): Every 30 days, or before any hot work in the monitored area.
  - Full calibration (zero + span): Every 6 months, or after any alarm event.
  - Sensor element replacement: Every 24 months, or when span calibration cannot be achieved
    within ±5% of reference gas value after two consecutive attempts.

4.2.2 Accelerated Calibration Triggers
The following conditions require IMMEDIATE calibration regardless of schedule:
  (a) Any alarm event above 25% LEL.
  (b) Sensor exposure to silicone vapours, lead compounds, halogenated solvents, or H2S
      concentrations above 200 ppm — these compounds cause catalytic bead poisoning and
      permanently reduce sensor sensitivity.
  (c) Sensor readings that drift more than ±3% LEL from known zero in a clean atmosphere.
  (d) Any physical impact or moisture ingress to the sensor housing.

4.3 CALIBRATION PROCEDURE

4.3.1 Pre-Calibration Safety Requirements
IMPORTANT: Before performing calibration in a live area:
  (a) Obtain a Hot Work / Gas Work permit from the area supervisor.
  (b) Verify the area reading is below 10% LEL using a portable gas detector BEFORE
      connecting calibration gas or opening the sensor housing.
  (c) Inform the shift operator and control room of planned calibration activity.
  (d) Have a second person present as safety observer.

4.3.2 Zero Calibration
  Step 1: Connect certified zero gas (synthetic clean air, <1 ppm HC) at 0.5 L/min.
  Step 2: Allow reading to stabilise (minimum 2 minutes).
  Step 3: If reading is within ±1% LEL of zero, no adjustment required. Log and sign off.
  Step 4: If reading is outside ±1% LEL, adjust zero potentiometer per Figure 4-3.
  Step 5: Record pre-adjustment and post-adjustment readings on Calibration Record Form CF-01.

4.3.3 Span Calibration
  Step 1: Use certified reference gas at 50% LEL methane in nitrogen balance.
          IMPORTANT: Reference gas cylinder must have a valid calibration certificate
          dated within the past 12 months from an accredited laboratory.
  Step 2: Connect at 0.5 L/min via the sensor inlet cap (Figure 4-4).
  Step 3: Allow reading to stabilise (minimum 3 minutes).
  Step 4: Adjust span potentiometer until display reads 50.0% ±1.0% LEL.
  Step 5: If span cannot be achieved within ±5% of reference after two attempts,
          sensor element requires replacement (see Section 4.5).

4.4 RECOGNISING PRE-FAILURE DRIFT

4.4.1 Early Warning Signs
Sensor degradation typically follows a predictable pattern:
  Phase 1 (Early): Zero drift increases gradually. Span calibration achievable.
  Phase 2 (Mid):   Zero drift >2% LEL. Span requires multiple adjustments.
  Phase 3 (Late):  Span cannot be achieved. Sensor may give intermittent high readings.

CRITICAL: A sensor in Phase 3 is dangerous. It may alarm at artificially high readings
(causing unnecessary evacuations) OR may lose sensitivity and fail to alarm when gas is
actually present. Both failure modes are safety-critical.

4.4.2 Field Identification of Pre-Failure Drift
If an operator reports an "unexplained" high reading that cannot be confirmed by a portable
detector, and the area gas reading with portable detection is significantly lower than the
fixed detector reading, this is strong evidence of Phase 2 or Phase 3 sensor drift.
DO NOT dismiss unexplained high readings as sensor error — always investigate the actual
gas condition in the area AND the sensor calibration status.

4.5 SENSOR ELEMENT REPLACEMENT

Sensor element (Draeger Part No. 4511545):
  Recommended replacement interval: 24 months from installation date.
  Required replacement conditions: Failed span calibration (see 4.3.3), physical damage,
  confirmed poisoning exposure (see 4.2.2b), or reading anomalies per 4.4.

Replacement Procedure:
  Step 1: Power down the detector. Confirm area is safe (<10% LEL with portable).
  Step 2: Remove sensor cap (3 screws, M4 Torx). Disconnect sensor plug.
  Step 3: Install new sensor element. Note installation date on housing label.
  Step 4: Power on. Allow 4-hour warm-up before first calibration.
  Step 5: Perform full zero + span calibration (Section 4.3).
  Step 6: Record new sensor installation date in equipment logbook.

4.6 TROUBLESHOOTING — ELEVATED READINGS

Symptom: Sustained reading >15% LEL in area where no gas source expected.
Possible causes (investigate in this order):
  1. Actual gas leak present in monitored area — use portable detector to confirm.
     DO NOT assume sensor error until area is confirmed gas-free by portable detection.
  2. Sensor drift / calibration required — check calibration date. If overdue, calibrate.
  3. Sensor poisoning — check history of chemical exposures in area.
  4. Environmental interference (humidity, temperature extremes) — check conditions.

WARNING: Never acknowledge and silence a gas alarm without first investigating causes 1-4.
